Background
Mohammad Babar Azam was born on 15 October 1994 in Lahore, Punjab, Pakistan. He hails from a cricketing family — three of his cousins, Kamran Akmal, Umar Akmal, and Adnan Akmal, all played international cricket for Pakistan.

A technically gifted right-handed batsman, Babar is renowned for his elegant cover drives, precise footwork, and consistency across all formats. He became Pakistan's captain across formats and reached No. 1 rankings in ODI, T20I, and Test cricket simultaneously — a rare feat in the modern era.

Babar Azam is frequently compared to Virat Kohli and Joe Root, and is widely regarded as one of the finest batsmen of his generation. His fluent strokeplay draws admiration from legends including Sir Vivian Richards and Sachin Tendulkar.
Key Achievements
🏆Reached ICC No. 1 ranking simultaneously in ODI, T20I, and Test cricket — one of very few batsmen to achieve this.
🌟Became Pakistan's all-time leading ODI run-scorer, surpassing legends like Saeed Anwar and Inzamam-ul-Haq.
📈Scored 20+ ODI centuries at a pace rivalling Virat Kohli's record-breaking form at the same career stage.
🎖️Named ICC Men's ODI Cricketer of the Year multiple times and a regular in ICC's World Test XI.
🦁Led Pakistan to the ICC T20 World Cup semi-finals and consistently ranked among top 3 T20I batsmen globally.

Background
Meg Lanning was born on 25 March 1992 in Singapore to Australian parents and grew up in Melbourne, Australia. She made her Women's ODI debut at just 18 years of age in 2010, scoring a fifty in her very first match.

She became one of the youngest captains of the Australian women's team and led them to extraordinary dominance in international cricket. A compact right-handed batter known for her aggressive clean hitting, Lanning excels in both ODI and T20 formats.

Widely regarded as the greatest women's cricketer of her era, she led Australia to six ICC World Cup titles. In 2023, she announced her retirement, leaving behind an unmatched legacy in women's cricket history.
Key Achievements
🏆Led Australia to 6 ICC World Cup victories as captain — the most successful captaincy in women's cricket history.
🌟Scored a fifty on her ODI debut at age 18, one of the youngest to do so for Australia.
📈Held ICC Women's ODI rankings No. 1 spot for extended period; among the highest ODI run-scorers in women's cricket history.
🎖️Named ICC Women's Cricketer of the Year multiple times and received the Belinda Clark Award — Australia's highest women's cricket honour.
🦁Inducted into the Cricket Australia Hall of Fame on retirement in 2023.
